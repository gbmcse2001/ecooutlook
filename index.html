GDP: Steady but Softening

A Decade of Expansion

Texarkana's economy has grown significantly over the past decade, from $5.2B in 2014 to $7.2B in 2024 — a 38% increase. Post-pandemic recovery drove exceptional growth of 7–8% annually in 2021–2023, though the pace is now easing to a more sustainable 6.4%.

Demographics

Who Lives in Texarkana?

Gender (2014–2024)
  • Stable 50/50 male-female split across all years
  • Both genders dipped in 2020 and have since recovered
Race & Ethnicity
  • ~72% White Non-Hispanic
  • 17% Black — exceeds Texas average
  • 4.8% Hispanic
Age Structure
  • Median age: 37.3
  • Skews older than Texas statewide
  • Larger share aged 55+ signals future workforce pressure
  • Above-65 share rising from 21% to 24% (2014–2024)
Education Gap: Only 16.6% of Texarkana residents hold a 4-year college degree, compared to 26.1% statewide — though the share has been gradually rising from 12% in 2014.
Labor Markets

Employment Trends

By Gender
  • Men consistently show higher employment rates
  • Both genders dipped in 2020; both have recovered
  • Female employment: ~62% in 2024
  • Male employment: ~67% in 2024
By Race
  • Hispanic residents: highest employment rates
  • Black residents: lowest (~50–57% range)
  • Gap warrants targeted workforce programs
By Education
  • College graduates: ~80% employment rate
  • No diploma: ~45% employment rate
  • Education is the single strongest predictor of employment

Key Takeaways

Summary

✦ Strengths
  • GDP growing 6–8% annually
  • Per-capita income up 41% since 2014
  • College attainment rising (12% → 17%)
  • Transportation sector expanding (+2.0 pp)
  • Affordable housing relative to state
⚠ Challenges to Watch
  • Degree rate half the state average
  • Incomes 23% below state median
  • Persistent racial income gaps
  • Significant gender wage gap
  • Aging workforce reducing labor supply
